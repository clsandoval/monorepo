# Shibuya Rooftop Photography Spots

Research compiled for urban photography in Shibuya, Tokyo.

---

## Free Access Spots

### Shibuya Hikarie Sky Lobby
- **Floor:** 11F
- **Address:** 2-21-1 Shibuya, Tokyo
- **Hours:** 11 a.m. - 9 p.m. daily
- **Cost:** Free
- **Best for:** Shibuya Crossing views, JR Yamanote Line entrance
- **Notes:** Some obstruction but uncrowded. Good for casual snaps without paying.

### Miyashita Park Rooftop
- **Location:** Above Miyashita Park / Rayard shopping complex
- **Cost:** Free
- **Best for:** Downtown Shibuya night lights, street-level urban vibe
- **Notes:** Has skatepark, bouldering wall. Great after sunset when city lights turn on.

### Shibuya Parco Rooftop Terrace
- **Floor:** Rooftop (access via indoor escalators)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Best for:** Low-to-mid height city views, intimate urban feel
- **Notes:** Open-air terrace with seating. External staircase for wandering.

### Shibuya Station (Keio Inokashira Line)
- **Location:** Station concourse windows
- **Cost:** Free
- **Best for:** Quick shots of Scramble Crossing through mesh windows
- **Notes:** Smartphone-friendly, mesh allows photography.

## Paid Observation Decks

### Shibuya Sky (Shibuya Scramble Square)
- **Floor:** 46F (Sky Gallery indoor) + 47F (Sky Stage open-air)
- **Height:** 230m / 229m
- **Hours:** 10 a.m. - 10:30 p.m. daily
- **Cost:**
  - Before 3 p.m.: 짜2,700
  - After 3 p.m.: 짜3,400
- **Best for:** 360-degree panoramic views, Mt. Fuji on clear days, night cityscapes
- **Photo spots:**
  - Glass escalator to rooftop
  - Sky Edge (glass corner)
  - Cloud Hammocks
- **Restrictions:** No tripods, no selfie sticks, no large equipment
- **Tips:** Book ahead for sunset slots (60-90 min before sunset recommended)

### MAGNET by Shibuya 109 - Crossing View / Mag's Park
- **Floor:** Rooftop (8F)
- **Hours:** 10 a.m. - 10 p.m. daily
- **Cost:** 짜1,800 (includes one drink)
- **Best for:** Direct aerial shots of Shibuya Scramble Crossing
- **Notes:**
  - CROSSING PHOTO service: 짜1,000 for 100-second photo session
  - Tripods possible at Mags Park Rooftop if not crowded
  - Perfect height - high enough for aerials, low enough for creative shots

### SHIBU NIWA (Tokyu Plaza Shibuya)
- **Floor:** 17F
- **Cost:** Free (rooftop garden)
- **Best for:** Southeast/north views - Scramble Crossing, Miyashita Park, Shibuya Stream
- **Notes:** Rooftop garden with trees and seating. "Oasis of nature" feel.

## Cafes & Restaurants with Views

### Starbucks Shibuya (QFRONT 2F)
- **Address:** QFRONT 2F, 5-1-21 Udagawacho, Shibuya
- **Hours:** 7 a.m. - 10:30 p.m.
- **Cost:** Purchase required
- **Best for:** Classic Shibuya Crossing shots
- **Notes:** Most common/crowded vantage point. Arrive early for window seats.

### Shunsai (Shibuya Excel Hotel Tokyu 25F)
- **Hours:** 11:30 a.m. - 3 p.m., 5:30 p.m. - 10 p.m.
- **Cost:** Restaurant pricing
- **Best for:** Elevated dining with crossing views
- **Notes:** Japanese restaurant, serene atmosphere. Less crowded than cafes.

### L'Occitane Cafe Shibuya
- **Floors:** 2F and 3F window seating
- **Hours:** 10 a.m. - 11 p.m.
- **Cost:** Cafe pricing
- **Notes:** Partially tree-obstructed views. Pleasant atmosphere.

## Nearby Hidden Gems (Not Shibuya but Close)

### Carrot Tower (Sangenjaya)
- **Floor:** 26F observation deck
- **Cost:** Free
- **Best for:** Different perspective from central Tokyo, Mt. Fuji on clear days
- **Notes:** Local favorite. Casual bar/music in evenings. Low tourist traffic.

### Harakado Rooftop (Harajuku)
- **Location:** Top of Harakado lifestyle complex
- **Cost:** Free
- **Best for:** Wide views of Shibuya and Shinjuku skyline
- **Notes:** Local cafes/food stalls. Weekend markets. Newer spot.

### With Harajuku Rooftop Garden
- **Location:** Above Harajuku Station (2F-3F)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Best for:** Small hidden oasis, quiet escape from crowds
- **Notes:** Opened 2020 with station revamp. Lesser known.

## Photography Tips

### Best Times
- **Golden hour / Blue hour:** Evening transition is prime time for Shibuya
- **Night:** After 6-7 p.m. when neon lights fully activate
- **Rainy days:** Wet streets create beautiful reflections

### Gear Notes
- Most observation decks prohibit tripods and selfie sticks
- Compact cameras and smartphones are always fine
- Mags Park Rooftop allows tripods when not crowded

### Scramble Crossing Tips
- Peak crossing times: Every 2-3 minutes during rush hour
- Light changes create natural pacing for shots
- Higher vantage points = better pedestrian flow patterns
